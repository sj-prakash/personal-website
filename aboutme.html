How I got into open-source

It all started when I first heard about LibreOffice, an open-source alternative to Microsoft Office.

Then I started to delve more into open-source operating systems and softwares. Exploring distributions like Ubuntu and libre softwares broadened my understanding of the movement's impact on software and user freedoms.

At first I kind of ignored it and went straight into installing Ubuntu, but then my curiosity got the best of me and I looked it up to what it was.

I was instantly hooked (It was something like self-hosted email) and began installing it on my laptop. Now please note that I had no prior experience of installing OS at all.

That very night as I was installing it, I also started to explore open-source custom roms for Andriod phones.

I went and pulled out my old Oneplus X that I had lying around and installed CyanogenMod Rom on that.

During this journey I stumbled upon the most secure custom rom for Andriod - LineageOS.

I had a lot of fun with it and it was a great learning experience. I was able to install many apps and servies that I had no idea existed before.

Thus, began the exploration of FOSS softwares and OS.
